abstract: "How can crises be met without fiscal transfers? Using proprietary transaction and credit data, we exploit discontinuous eligibility for Belgium's 2020 mortgage moratorium based on pre-crisis delinquency and liquid-asset thresholds. Near the cutoff, eligible households cut mortgage payments and raise non-durable and services spending, with the largest gains among low-liquidity borrowers, compressing consumption inequality and reducing income inequality during the program. A life-cycle heterogeneous-agent model with long-term, defaultable mortgages, disciplined by balance sheets and average spending, matches out-of-sample heterogeneity. In general equilibrium, moratoria stabilize house prices and bank net worth; broad extensions can weaken banks, tighten credit, and reduce demand."
